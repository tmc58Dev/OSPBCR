Patient Information Form

Patient Information Form

PBCR Odisha_Proforma

The Odisha State Population Based Cancer Registry (OSPBCR) has developed a standardized Patient Information Form to facilitate systematic collection of cancer-related data across the state. The form serves as the primary tool for recording demographic, clinical, diagnostic, treatment, and follow-up information required for population-based cancer registration.

The form captures essential patient details, including demographic characteristics, contact information, residential location, hospital details, and Aadhaar number to support accurate identification and follow-up. Clinical information collected includes the date and basis of diagnosis, primary site of cancer, stage at diagnosis, histological findings, and ICD-O-3-based classification to ensure standardized reporting.

Comprehensive information on cancer treatment, including the type of treatment received, treatment status, and combinations of therapeutic modalities, is documented to facilitate analysis of treatment patterns and outcomes. The form also records vital status, age and date of death where applicable, enabling long-term patient follow-up and survival analysis.

The standardized data collection format supports uniform reporting across participating healthcare facilities, improves data quality and completeness, and strengthens the accuracy of cancer incidence, treatment, and survival estimates. It provides the foundation for evidence-based cancer surveillance, research, programme planning, and policy development under the Odisha State Population Based Cancer Registry.

Standard Operating Procedure

Standard Operating Procedure

Odisha PBCR SOP

The Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) has been developed to provide uniform guidelines for the implementation of the Odisha State Population Based Cancer Registry (OSPBCR). The registry is an initiative of the Department of Health and Family Welfare, Government of Odisha, with technical support from Tata Memorial Centre (TMC), Mumbai, and the National Institute of Science Education and Research (NISER), Jatni, Odisha.

The SOP serves as a reference manual for all personnel involved in cancer registration across the thirty districts of Odisha, ensuring standardized procedures for data collection, case abstraction, coding, and reporting. It aims to maintain high-quality, complete, and consistent cancer registry data across all participating centres.

Registry data collection and abstraction are carried out using the web-based Setu application, which incorporates a digitized version of the standardized registry proforma. The one-page proforma has been designed to capture essential variables that are routinely available during data collection, thereby promoting efficient and accurate reporting.

The manual provides detailed guidance on each data variable collected by the registry and supports registry staff in maintaining uniform data recording practices. Users are encouraged to study the SOP thoroughly before commencing registry activities and to seek guidance from supervisors and Tata Memorial Centre experts whenever technical clarification or operational support is required.

Setu Platform Guide

Setu Platform Guide

Setu User Manual

The Odisha State Population-Based Cancer Registry (OSPBCR) is an initiative of the Department of Health and Family Welfare, Government of Odisha, established to systematically collect and manage cancer data across the state. The registry aims to strengthen cancer surveillance and support evidence-based planning for cancer prevention and control.

The Setu web-based application has been developed as the official data management platform for the OSPBCR. It facilitates standardized collection, storage, and management of cancer registry data, ensuring uniform reporting and improved data quality across participating institutions.

All cancer-treating hospitals, District Headquarters Hospitals (DHHs), Primary Health Centres (PHCs), and other healthcare facilities involved in the diagnosis and treatment of cancer patients in Odisha are responsible for entering patient information into the Setu platform. This coordinated reporting mechanism supports comprehensive statewide cancer registration and strengthens the registry's ability to generate reliable estimates of the cancer burden.
