Executive Summary

The Odisha State Population Based Cancer Registry (OSPBCR) is an initiative of the Department of Health and Family Welfare, Government of Odisha, to systematically document the cancer burden across the state, covering a population of 45 million people. The registry is being implemented from 2026 onwards with technical support from Tata Memorial Centre, Mumbai, and the National Institute for Science Education and Research, Jatni, Odisha.

Training workshops for district health officials from all 30 districts of Odisha were conducted in two batches, with the first batch held from 7th to 9th January 2026 for 16 districts and the second batch from 12th to 14th January 2026 for the remaining 14 districts. A total of 115 participants, including 26 District Public Health Officers (DPHO) and Additional District Public Health Officers (ADPHO), attended the workshops.

The training covered core concepts of cancer registration, case abstraction procedures, ICD-O-3 coding, use of the "Setu" web-based data management tool, follow-up techniques, and the Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) of the PBCR. Practical demonstrations were conducted by the staff of Khordha PBCR, and pre- and post-tests indicated a strong understanding of registry-related concepts among the participants and their readiness to support registry activities.
