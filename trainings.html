District Wise Hospital Training

Executive Summary

The Odisha State Population Based Cancer Registry (OSPBCR) is an initiative of the Department of Health and Family Welfare, Government of Odisha, to systematically document the cancer burden across the state, covering a population of 45 million people. The registry is being implemented from 2026 onwards with technical support from Tata Memorial Centre, Mumbai, and the National Institute for Science Education and Research, Jatni, Odisha.

Training workshops for district health officials from all 30 districts of Odisha were conducted in two batches, with the first batch held from 7th to 9th January 2026 for 16 districts and the second batch from 12th to 14th January 2026 for the remaining 14 districts. A total of 115 participants, including 26 District Public Health Officers (DPHO) and Additional District Public Health Officers (ADPHO), attended the workshops.

The training covered core concepts of cancer registration, case abstraction procedures, ICD-O-3 coding, use of the "Setu" web-based data management tool, follow-up techniques, and the Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) of the PBCR. Practical demonstrations were conducted by the staff of Khordha PBCR, and pre- and post-tests indicated a strong understanding of registry-related concepts among the participants and their readiness to support registry activities.

Private Hospital Training

Executive Summary

The Odisha State Population Based Cancer Registry (OSPBCR) is an initiative of the Department of Health and Family Welfare, Government of Odisha, to systematically document the cancer burden across the state, covering a population of 45 million people. The registry is being implemented from 2026 onwards with technical support from Tata Memorial Centre, Mumbai, and the National Institute for Science Education and Research (NISER), Jatni, Odisha.

A training workshop for officials from major cancer treatment facilities and tertiary care centres in Bhubaneswar and Cuttack was conducted from 27th January to 3rd February 2026. The workshop was also attended by the newly recruited OSPBCR team, comprising one State Coordinator, five Zonal Coordinators, and one Programmer, with a total of 40 participants taking part in the programme.

The training covered the fundamental principles of population-based cancer registration, case abstraction procedures, ICD-O-3 coding, use of the "SETU" web-based cancer registration system, follow-up techniques, and the Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for PBCR implementation. Expert faculty delivered lectures on cancer management and clinical terminologies to strengthen participants' understanding of data abstraction, while practical demonstrations were conducted by the Khordha PBCR team. Pre- and post-training assessments indicated a strong understanding of registry-related concepts and readiness among participants to support registry activities.

The workshop coincided with the observance of World Cancer Day 2026, during which the Department of Health and Family Welfare, Government of Odisha, officially launched the Odisha State Population Based Cancer Registry at the National Institute of Science Education and Research (NISER), Bhubaneswar. The programme was inaugurated by Dr. Nilakantha Mishra, Director of Public Health, Government of Odisha, and was presided over by Dr. A. Srinivasan, Dean of Faculty Affairs, NISER, marking an important milestone in strengthening cancer surveillance across the state.
